# AI Image Generation

I don't want to get too philosophical, however I do think this is an inherently philosophical topic when you start getting into the weeds of it. I'll also not be diving into the specifics of the tech either as I'm not well versed enough.

## What Is Image Generation?

Image generation is the process of using computers running an "AI" to create an image from thin air using a provided prompt. This process essentially uses a Model[^2] to generate the image.

For example, the prompt:

> "A lone man wearing a red suit walking down a futuristic city street in the year 2900"

## Thought 1: How to spot AI Images

I think this is something that needs to be shared far and wide before it's too late. How can someone who knows nothing about AI image generation spot an AI generated image and why is this important?

This isn't actually to hard at this point in time. There is a good chance we will not be able to tell the difference between a generated image and a photograph in the near future. **So if it 2050, ignore this advice.**

1. **Telltale Signs**: Hands and eyes. AI isn't great at reproducing these so look out for extra fingers, missing fingernails, square pupils and wonky eyes. Look at the details and you will soon see errors.
2. **Context Clues**: AI often misses the mark with context. Would a male lion carry a cub in its mouth? Do you get sunflowers growing in the Atacama desert? Consider the context of the situation.
3. **Resolution**: Less reliable, but useful. If the image isn't high resolution, it could be AI. Most modern phones and cameras take high res images, so why would a paparazzi shot of David Attenborough swinging a cat by it's legs look like it was taken on a potato?
4. **Text**: AI is getting better at text but in many cases it just created gibberish. Look at the words and numbers, if they look alien, the image is probably fake...unless its a screen grab from the Matrix.

These will never get you there all the time, but they will help you spot the obvious errors. It's worth noting that people are usually created well, its the other stuff like animals and small items that are not. The key is to be perceptive to these errors and not glance over them.
